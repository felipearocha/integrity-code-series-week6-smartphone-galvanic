1. Macro Scale — Laplace Equation (Electrolyte Domain)

1.1 Potential distribution in the thin film

In the thin electrolyte film, assuming electroneutrality and negligible convection, the electrolyte potential $\phi$ satisfies current continuity: $$ \nabla\!\cdot\!\bigl(\kappa\,\nabla\phi\bigr) \;=\; 0 $$ with $\phi$ the electrolyte potential [V] and $\kappa$ the electrolyte conductivity [S/m]. For a film of thickness $\delta_e$ this reduces to a 2D problem on the port surface (8.34 mm × 2.56 mm USB-C receptacle cross section).

SOURCE: Laplace equation / current continuity for an ohmic electrolyte (standard electrochemistry, constant $\kappa$). Electrolyte: 0.5% NaCl sweat proxy, $\kappa \approx 1.5$ S/m [ASSUMED proxy].

Code: src/laplace_solver.py → solve_laplace_2d, extract_boundary_currents.

Boundary conditions: Butler-Volmer nonlinear-Neumann flux at each metal surface; zero-flux Neumann ($\partial\phi/\partial n = 0$) at the plastic insulator housing; a 5 V bias offset on the contact pins under charging.

2. Micro Scale — Butler-Volmer Kinetics

2.1 Interfacial current density

At each metal surface $k$ the interfacial current density follows Butler-Volmer electrode kinetics: $$ j_k \;=\; j_{0,k}\left\{\exp\!\left[\frac{\alpha_{a,k}\,F\,\eta_k}{R\,T}\right] - \exp\!\left[-\frac{\alpha_{c,k}\,F\,\eta_k}{R\,T}\right]\right\} $$ with overpotential $$ \eta_k \;=\; \phi_{\mathrm{metal},k} - \phi_{\mathrm{electrolyte}} - E_{\mathrm{eq},k} $$ where $j_{0,k}$ is the exchange current density [A/m²], $\alpha_{a,k},\alpha_{c,k}$ the anodic/cathodic transfer coefficients, and $E_{\mathrm{eq},k}$ the equilibrium potential of metal $k$.

SOURCE: Butler-Volmer electrode-kinetics equation (standard electrochemistry). Constants $F = 96485$ C/mol, $R = 8.314$ J·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹ (physical constants); $T$ uniform at 35 °C (body-contact). $E_{\mathrm{eq},k}$ from the materials table (vs SHE). Exchange current densities $j_{0,k}$ are [ASSUMED] order-of-magnitude estimates for thin-film NaCl and are the target of the inverse module (§6).

Code: src/butler_volmer.py → butler_volmer_current, tafel_anodic, tafel_cathodic, compute_metal_current.

3. Oxide Film Growth & Effective Kinetics

3.1 Protective-oxide resistance

A protective oxide film builds resistance in proportion to the anodic charge passed, feeding back into the effective exchange current density and coupling the micro and temporal scales: $$ R_{\mathrm{oxide},k}(t) \;=\; R_{\mathrm{oxide},0,k} \;+\; \beta_k\!\int j_{\mathrm{anodic},k}\,\mathrm{d}t $$
$\beta_k$ (oxide growth rate) is an [ASSUMED] constitutive parameter requiring calibration; oxide films are taken as compact and following linear growth with charge passed. Au dissolution is treated as negligible under normal conditions (included for completeness).

SOURCE: linear (charge-proportional) oxide-growth constitutive model [ASSUMED]; $R_{\mathrm{oxide},0,k}$ initial film resistance.

Code: src/butler_volmer.py → effective_j0.

4. Temporal Evolution — Faradaic Mass Loss

4.1 Dissolution and thickness loss

Mass loss per unit area of metal $k$ follows Faraday's law of electrolysis, driven by the anodic current density: $$ \frac{\mathrm{d}m_k}{\mathrm{d}t} \;=\; \frac{M_k}{n_k\,F}\,j_{\mathrm{anodic},k}, \qquad \frac{\mathrm{d}h_k}{\mathrm{d}t} \;=\; \frac{1}{\rho_k}\,\frac{\mathrm{d}m_k}{\mathrm{d}t} $$ with $M_k$ the molar mass [kg/mol], $n_k$ the electrons transferred per atom dissolved, and $\rho_k$ the density [kg/m³].

SOURCE: Faraday's law of electrolysis. $M_k$, $n_k$, $\rho_k$ from the materials table (handbook values).

Code: src/temporal_evolution.py → rhs_temporal, simulate_charge_cycles, estimate_port_lifetime.

5. Galvanic Coupling — Mixed-Potential Constraint

5.1 Charge-balance at the couple potential

At the mixed (couple) potential the total anodic current equals the total cathodic current over the exposed areas $A_k$: $$ \sum_k A_k\,j_{\mathrm{anodic},k}\bigl(\phi_{\mathrm{couple}}\bigr) \;=\; \sum_k A_k\,j_{\mathrm{cathodic},k}\bigl(\phi_{\mathrm{couple}}\bigr) $$ The mixed potential $\phi_{\mathrm{couple}}$ is the root of the net-current equation; area ratios drive the local dissolution rate of the anodic member.

SOURCE: mixed-potential theory (charge-conservation over coupled electrodes, standard electrochemistry).

Code: src/galvanic_coupling.py → net_galvanic_current, solve_mixed_potential, compute_area_ratio_sensitivity.

6. Inverse Estimation — Bayesian MCMC

6.1 Gaussian likelihood, uniform log-prior, Metropolis-Hastings

Exchange current densities $j_{0,k}$ are estimated from sparse current observations by Metropolis-Hastings sampling of the posterior. The likelihood is Gaussian in the measured couple current, the prior uniform in $\log_{10} j_0$ space: $$ \ln L \;=\; -\tfrac{1}{2}\sum_i \frac{\bigl(I_{\mathrm{pred},i} - I_{\mathrm{obs},i}\bigr)^2}{\sigma_i^{2}}, \qquad \pi\bigl(\log_{10} j_0\bigr) \;=\; \begin{cases} \text{const}, & \text{within bounds}\\[2pt] -\infty, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$ $$ \ln \pi(\theta\mid \mathcal{D}) \;=\; \ln L \;+\; \ln \pi(\theta) \;+\; \text{const} $$

SOURCE: Gaussian-likelihood Bayesian inference with Metropolis-Hastings MCMC (standard formulation, as implemented). Proposal: Gaussian in $\log_{10}$ space; priors are the per-metal $j_0$ bounds in the config.

Code: src/inverse_bayesian.py → log_likelihood, log_prior, run_mcmc, compute_posterior_statistics.
